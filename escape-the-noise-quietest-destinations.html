How to Protect Your Peace

1

Check the Map First

Before booking, look up your destination on the DOT Noise Map. Green is good. Red is a warning.

2

Look for Rail Lines

Open Google Maps and check for railroad tracks. Train horns hit 110 dB. Many "quiet" towns have active freight lines.

3

Ask About Room Location

Call ahead. Request a room away from the road, on the upper floors, or facing away from any highway.

4

Go Shoulder Season

Tourist traffic is noise. Visit in spring or fall when the crowds‚Äîand their cars‚Äîare gone.

Data Source

All noise data comes from the U.S. Department of Transportation's Bureau of Transportation Statistics National Transportation Noise Map (2020 dataset).

What Was Measured

  • Combined aviation, highway, and rail noise levels
  • Distance to nearest commercial airport
  • Distance to nearest interstate highway
  • Population density and environmental type

Scoring Formula

  • Base score calculated from population + distance metrics
  • Every destination visually verified against the DOT noise map
  • +5 to +10 bonus for exceptionally quiet readings (<45 dB)
  • -10 to -50 penalty for elevated noise (>50 dB)

Why Map Verification Matters

Formula-based rankings miss hidden noise sources: active rail lines, flight paths, acoustic canyon effects, and seasonal traffic. We checked every destination against the actual federal noise measurements to catch what formulas miss.
